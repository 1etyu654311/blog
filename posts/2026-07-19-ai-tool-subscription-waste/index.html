The subscription bloat problem

Here's how it happens. You sign up for ChatGPT ($20). Then someone recommends Claude ($20). Then you need images, so Midjourney ($10). Then Jasper for "better marketing copy" ($49). Notion AI for notes ($10). Canva Pro for designs ($13). descrybed for podcasts ($15). Before you know it, you're paying for 10+ tools and only regularly use 2 or 3.

I tracked my own subscriptions for 3 months. Here's what I found:

  • 12 active subscriptions totaling $247/month
  • Only 3 tools used weekly — the rest were "nice to have" experiments
  • 80% of features overlapped between tools I was paying for
  • $197/month wasted on tools I touched fewer than 3 times

Why tool recommendation lists make it worse

Most "best AI tools of 2026" articles are affiliate-driven content. The writer gets a commission when you click and subscribe. They have zero incentive to tell you that 80% of the tools on their list overlap in functionality.

The tool you already have probably does the thing you need. You just haven't explored it deeply enough.

I fell for this too. Every shiny new AI tool launches with a slick landing page, a generous free tier, and an army of affiliate marketers pushing it. Three weeks later, you've added another $20/month to your stack without realizing the tool you already had did 90% of the same job.

The 3-tool setup that actually works

After testing 15+ tools extensively, here's the setup that covers 95% of real-world use cases:

1. Claude (writing) — $20/month

Best for: long-form content, analysis, nuanced writing, document review. Claude's 200K context window means you can feed it entire books, codebases, or research papers. The writing quality is noticeably more natural than ChatGPT, especially for anything over 1000 words.

2. ChatGPT (research + general) — Free tier

Best for: quick questions, brainstorming, coding help, data analysis. The free tier gives you GPT-4o access with message limits that are generous enough for most people. If you hit limits daily, the $20 Plus tier is worth it — but start free.

3. Cursor (code) — Free tier

Best for: software development. Cursor gives you 2,000 completions/month on the free tier. It integrates Claude and GPT-4 directly into VS Code. If you code professionally, this single tool replaces Copilot, Tabnine, and any other AI coding assistant.

Total cost: $20-40/month instead of $200+. That's $1,800+ saved per year.

How to audit your stack in 15 minutes

If you're reading this and realizing you might be overpaying, here's a quick audit:

  1. Open your credit card statement from last month
  2. List every AI and software subscription — include the ones you forgot about
  3. For each one, ask: "Did I use this in the last 7 days?"
  4. If no — cancel immediately. Don't "keep it just in case"
  5. For maybes — set a calendar reminder for 30 days. If you still haven't used it, cancel
  6. Replace your remaining tools with the 3-tool setup above

The whole audit takes 15 minutes. I saved $168/month doing this. That's a car payment. That's a weekend trip. That's $2,000 a year back in your pocket.

You don't need 12 tools. You need 3 good ones and the discipline to use them well.
